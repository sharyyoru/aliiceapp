## SOAP Note Format

### Subjective (S)
Patient-reported information:
- **Chief Complaint (CC)**: Main reason for visit in patient's words
- **History of Present Illness (HPI)**: Onset, location, duration, character, aggravating/alleviating factors, radiation, timing, severity (OLDCARTS)
- **Review of Systems (ROS)**: Relevant positive and negative findings
- **Past Medical History (PMH)**: Previous conditions, surgeries, hospitalizations
- **Medications**: Current medications with dosages
- **Allergies**: Drug allergies and reactions
- **Social History**: Smoking, alcohol, occupation if relevant
- **Family History**: Relevant genetic/familial conditions

### Objective (O)
Clinician-observed findings:
- **Vital Signs**: BP, HR, Temp, RR, SpO2, Weight
- **Physical Examination**: Systematic findings by body system
- **Skin Assessment**: Texture, elasticity, pigmentation, lesions
- **Treatment Areas**: Specific anatomical locations examined
- **Photos**: Reference to clinical photography if taken

### Assessment (A)
Clinical conclusions:
- **Primary Diagnosis**: Main condition being treated
- **Differential Diagnoses**: Other conditions considered
- **ICD-10 Codes**: Diagnostic codes for billing
- **Severity/Staging**: If applicable

### Plan (P)
Treatment approach:
- **Procedures**: Treatments performed or planned
- **Medications**: Prescriptions with dosage, route, frequency, duration
- **Patient Education**: Instructions given to patient
- **Follow-up**: Next appointment timing and purpose
- **Referrals**: Specialist consultations if needed
- **Labs/Imaging**: Tests ordered

## Common Procedures & Terminology

### Injectable Treatments
- **Neuromodulator injection**: Botulinum toxin for muscle relaxation
- **Dermal filler injection**: HA or other fillers for volume
- **Mesotherapy**: Microinjections of vitamins/medications
- **PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma)**: Autologous blood-derived treatment
- **Kybella**: Deoxycholic acid for submental fat

### Laser & Light Treatments
- **IPL (Intense Pulsed Light)**: Photofacial, pigment, vessels
- **Fractional CO2**: Ablative resurfacing
- **Fractional Erbium**: Less aggressive resurfacing
- **Nd:YAG**: Vascular, hair removal, deep heating
- **Q-switched**: Tattoo removal, pigment
- **Pico laser**: Picosecond laser for pigment/tattoo
- **LED therapy**: Red/blue light for healing/acne

### Skin Procedures
- **Chemical peel**: Glycolic, TCA, Jessner, phenol
- **Microneedling**: Collagen induction therapy
- **RF microneedling**: Morpheus8, Vivace, Potenza
- **Dermaplaning**: Manual exfoliation
- **HydraFacial**: Hydradermabrasion
- **Microdermabrasion**: Crystal/diamond exfoliation

### Body Treatments
- **CoolSculpting**: Cryolipolysis fat reduction
- **Emsculpt**: HIFEM muscle toning
- **Kybella**: Deoxycholic acid injection
- **RF body contouring**: TruSculpt, Exilis
- **Laser lipolysis**: SmartLipo, SlimLipo

## Follow-Up Guidelines

### Post-Neurotoxin
- Avoid lying down for 4 hours
- No strenuous exercise for 24 hours
- Results visible in 3-14 days
- Follow-up at 2 weeks for touch-up if needed
- Duration: 3-4 months typically

### Post-Filler
- Ice as needed for swelling
- Avoid strenuous exercise for 24-48 hours
- Avoid extreme heat/cold for 2 weeks
- Massage if instructed
- Follow-up at 2 weeks
- Watch for: Severe pain, vision changes, skin blanching

### Post-Laser
- Apply prescribed ointment
- Avoid sun exposure
- Use SPF 30+ daily
- Expect redness/swelling
- Follow-up varies by treatment

### Post-Peel
- Keep skin moisturized
- Do not pick at peeling skin
- Avoid sun exposure
- Follow specific peel protocol
- Follow-up in 1-2 weeks

## Documentation Best Practices

### For Injectables
Document:
- Product name and lot number
- Total units/syringes used
- Specific injection sites
- Technique used (linear, bolus, fanning)
- Patient tolerance
- Immediate results
- Post-procedure photos
- Instructions given

### For Laser Treatments
Document:
- Device and settings (energy, pulse width)
- Treatment areas and passes
- Endpoint achieved
- Patient response
- Post-treatment care
- Follow-up plan

### For All Consultations
- Informed consent obtained
- Photos taken (with consent)
- Treatment expectations discussed
- Risks and complications reviewed
- Post-care instructions provided
- Follow-up scheduled

## Emergency Recognition

### Vascular Occlusion Signs
- Blanching (white skin)
- Severe pain disproportionate to procedure
- Dusky or mottled skin
- Vision changes (ophthalmic artery)
- Requires immediate intervention with hyaluronidase

### Allergic Reaction Signs
- Hives, itching
- Swelling beyond treatment area
- Difficulty breathing
- May require epinephrine, antihistamines

### Infection Signs
- Increasing pain after 48 hours
- Fever
- Spreading redness
- Pus or discharge
- Requires antibiotics, possible drainage

## Voice Commands for Aliice

The doctor can use these commands during consultation:

| Command | Action |
|---------|--------|
| "Aliice, note that..." | Add specific note to transcript |
| "Aliice, summarize" | Read back key points |
| "Aliice, what codes?" | Suggest ICD-10 codes |
| "Aliice, medication list" | List medications discussed |
| "Aliice, treatment areas" | Confirm areas treated |
| "Aliice, follow-up" | State follow-up plan |
| "Aliice, stop" | End transcription |
| "Aliice, repeat" | Read back last statement |
